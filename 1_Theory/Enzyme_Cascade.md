## **2. Architecture of the Cascade**

The cascade consists of **four primary enzyme classes**, each contributing a distinct transformation function:

### **2.1 Oxidoreductases**

- Drive electron transfer
- Enable redox cycling
- Initiate breakdown of complex organics
- Support methane oxidation and CO₂ conversion pathways

### **2.2 Hydrolases**

- Cleave chemical bonds using water
- Break down cellulose, lignin, proteins, lipids
- Release simple sugars and amino acids

### **2.3 Transferases**

- Move functional groups between molecules
- Enable biosynthesis of new compounds
- Support nitrogen assimilation and amino acid formation

### **2.4 Lyases**

- Break bonds without hydrolysis
- Enable ring-opening reactions
- Support heavy metal chelation and transformation

Together, these enzymes form a **cooperative metabolic chain**.

## **3. Reaction Pathway Structure**

The cascade follows a generalized reaction sequence:

$$ S_0 \xrightarrow{E_1} S_1 \xrightarrow{E_2} S_2 \xrightarrow{E_3} S_3 \xrightarrow{E_4} P $$

Where:

- (S_0) = initial substrate
- (E_1, E_2, E_3, E_4) = enzyme classes
- (P) = final product (nutrient, flux molecule, or transformed element)

Each step increases:

- Reaction rate
- Bioavailability
- Microbial uptake efficiency

This creates a **kinetically amplified metabolic loop**.

## **4. Kinetic Model**

The cascade is modeled using Michaelis–Menten kinetics extended to multi-step chains:

$$ v_i = \frac{V_{max,i} [S_i]}{K_{m,i} + [S_i]} $$

$$ \frac{dS_i}{dt} = -v_i + v_{i-1} $$

Where:

- (v_i) = reaction velocity of step (i)
- (V_{max,i}) = maximum rate
- (K_{m,i}) = substrate affinity
- (S_i) = substrate concentration at step (i)

This captures:

- Saturation effects
- Enzyme synergy
- Rate-limiting steps
- Feedback amplification

## **5. Feedback Amplification**

The cascade interacts with microbial metabolism through:

### **5.1 Positive Feedback**

### **5.2 Negative Feedback**

- Substrate depletion slows reaction rates
- Product accumulation can inhibit upstream enzymes

These feedbacks stabilize the system.
