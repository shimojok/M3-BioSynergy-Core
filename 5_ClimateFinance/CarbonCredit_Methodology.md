# Carbon Credit Methodology — Planetary OS

## 1. Purpose
This methodology defines how MBT55-based ecological improvements are converted into carbon credits under UNFCCC‑aligned MRV frameworks.

## 2. Scope
Applicable to:
- Soil carbon sequestration
- Methane reduction
- Nitrous oxide reduction
- Heavy metal remediation (co-benefit)
- Water retention and drought resilience (co-benefit)

## 3. Baseline Definition
Baseline emissions are established using:
- Historical soil carbon data
- Regional emission factors (IPCC Tier 1–3)
- Field GHG measurements (CH₄, CO₂, N₂O)
- Soil degradation indicators

## 4. Project Scenario
MBT55 deployment increases:
- Soil carbon (C_s)
- Nitrogen retention (N_s)
- Moisture (W)
- Microbial biomass (N)
- Enzyme activity (Vmax)

These changes reduce:
- CH₄ flux
- N₂O flux
- CO₂ emissions from decomposition

## 5. Quantification Equations

### 5.1 Soil Carbon


\[
\Delta C = C_{project} - C_{baseline}
\]



### 5.2 Methane Reduction


\[
ER_{CH_4} = (CH4_{baseline} - CH4_{project}) \times GWP_{CH_4}
\]



### 5.3 Nitrous Oxide Reduction


\[
ER_{N_2O} = (N2O_{baseline} - N2O_{project}) \times GWP_{N_2O}
\]



### 5.4 Total Credits


\[
Credits = \Delta C + ER_{CH_4} + ER_{N_2O}
\]



## 6. Monitoring Requirements
- Soil sampling (0–30 cm)
- GHG field sensors
- Moisture and temperature logs
- Microbial activity assays
- Satellite NDVI and biomass data

## 7. Verification
Third‑party verification follows:
- ISO 14064‑2
- IPCC 2006 Guidelines
- UNFCCC CDM/VERRA methodologies

## 8. Output
A verified carbon credit unit (tCO₂e) ready for:
- PBPE tokenization
- Carbon markets
- Government reporting
